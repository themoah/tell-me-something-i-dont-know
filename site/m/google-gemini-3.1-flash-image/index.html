Here is a fascinating fact that many people don't know:

Bananas are botanically considered berries, but strawberries are not.

This sounds backward, but it all comes down to the scientific definition of a berry.

In botany, a berry is a fleshy fruit that develops from a single flower with one ovary. Based on this precise definition:

  • Bananas qualify because they meet all the criteria: they are fleshy, develop from a single flower with one ovary, and (originally) contained multiple seeds (though the commercial bananas we eat have been bred to have only tiny, unnoticeable seeds).
  • Strawberries, on the other hand, do not fit the botanical definition. They are what's known as an "aggregate fruit." A strawberry develops from a single flower, but that flower has many separate ovaries. Each tiny "seed" on the outside of a strawberry is actually a separate fruit (called an achene), and the sweet, red part we love to eat is the swollen receptacle holding them all.

So, next time you're having a fruit salad, remember that the "berry" is the banana, and the "strawberry" is... well, an aggregate fruit!
