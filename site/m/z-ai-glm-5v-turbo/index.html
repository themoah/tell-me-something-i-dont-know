Here is a fact that usually blows people's minds:

If you take a standard deck of 52 playing cards and shuffle them thoroughly, the specific order of cards you are holding is almost certainly unique in the history of the universe.

Here is the math: There are 80,658,175,170,943,878,571,660,636,856,403,766,975,289,505,440,883,277,824,000,000,000,000 (roughly $8 \times 10^{67}$) possible ways to arrange a deck of cards.

To put that number in perspective:

  • There are only about $10^{80}$ atoms in the entire observable universe.
  • If you started shuffling a deck of cards once every second since the universe began (13.8 billion years ago), you still wouldn't have come close to seeing every possible combination yet. In fact, you wouldn't even be a billionth of a percent there.

So, statistically speaking, the order of cards in your hands right now has likely never been held by another human being in history.
