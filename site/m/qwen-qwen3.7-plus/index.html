That means sharks were swimming in the oceans long before Saturn had its rings.

3. Victorian fashion was literally toxic. Thousands of people, particularly children, suffered from chronic arsenic poisoning, and it is believed to have contributed to the death of Napoleon Bonaparte, whose bedroom in exile was famously decorated with the green wallpaper.

4. There is an opposite to Déjà vu, called Jamais vu. While déjà vu is the feeling that a new experience has happened before, jamais vu (French for "never seen") is the eerie sensation that something incredibly familiar is suddenly completely alien or new. A common example is "semantic satiation": if you write or say a very common word (like "door" or "spoon") over and over again for a minute, it will suddenly look and sound like gibberish, and your brain will temporarily lose its grasp on what the word means.

5. Wombats poop in cubes. This isn't just a fun trivia fact; it’s an evolutionary marvel. Wombats have very poor eyesight and use their droppings to mark their territory on top of rocks and logs. If their poop were round, it would roll away.
