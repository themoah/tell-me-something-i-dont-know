Here’s a little-known fact: Octopuses have three hearts and blue blood.

Two of their hearts pump blood through the gills, while the third circulates it to the rest of the body. Their blood is blue because it uses a copper-based protein called hemocyanin to carry oxygen—unlike our iron-based hemoglobin, which makes our blood red.

Even more interesting: when an octopus swims, the heart that delivers blood to the body stops beating, which is why they prefer crawling—it’s less tiring!

Here's a fun one:

A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus.

Venus takes about 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis, but only about 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun. So it literally completes a full trip around the Sun before it finishes spinning around once!

This is partly because Venus rotates backwards (retrograde rotation) compared to most planets, and very slowly at that. If you lived on Venus, you'd celebrate your birthday before you'd see a full sunrise-to-sunrise cycle.
